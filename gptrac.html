Who We Are & What We Do

The Great Plains Telehealth Resource and Assistance Center (gpTRAC) is a federally funded telehealth training and technical assistance center located at the University of Minnesota. We provide free resources, including training, tailored consultations, and print and web-based resources to healthcare organizations of all kinds seeking to design, implement, grow, sustain, and evaluate telehealth services.

GPTRAC IS A PROJECT OF THE UNIVERSITY OF MINNESOTA
INSTITUTE FOR HEALTH INFORMATICS

The Institute for Health Informatics (IHI) educates students, conducts research, and provides technical assistance services in the fields of biomedical and health informatics.  It focuses on the design, use, and evaluation of information systems that support and improve healthcare while protecting the safety and confidentiality of those who receive care. IHI is an interdisciplinary effort that encompasses work in a variety of settings from tertiary care hospitals to specialty clinics, mental health facilities, community clinics, nursing homes, and home care agencies.

Altru expands care and increases patient safety during COVID by offering video and phone visits, message-based eVisits, and provider-to-provider consultations. Altru is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

MercyOne Des Moines revitalizes the Virtually Integrated Care model to support patient care, improve efficiencies, and help families stay connected during the COVID-19 pandemic. MercyOne is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

Transformation of Tears: Telehealth Program Brings Mental Health Services to Rural School Students

Classroom Clinic, a telehealth company owned by a psychiatric nurse practitioner, pilots a school-based telehealth program to provide mental health services to rural students in Iowa. Classroom Clinic has consulted with gpTRAC.

The Time to Shine: Allina Health Dusts Off a Tabled Program to Meet Unique Needs During COVID-19

Allina's Home Hospital Care program helps keep patients safe, prevent the spread of COVID-19, and alleviate the demand on hospital capacity during the pandemic by providing high-quality care at home. Allina is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

A Web of Care for Our Elderly: Montgomery County Memorial Hospital Works With Nursing Facilities to Provide Safer Care

Montgomery County Memorial Hospital works with their nursing home and long-term care partners in Iowa to provide virtual services for their residents as well as rural elderly patients still living in their homes. MCMH is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

Understanding Both Sides of the Coin: Trista Barlow Experiences Telehealth as a Provider and User

Marshfield Clinic nurse sees how telehealth services have helped her patients during COVID, and she uses these services personally as a mother of two children who are enrolled in virtual counseling services. Marshfield Clinic is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

Doing Telehealth Before It Was Cool: Marshfield Clinic Health System Shares 23 Years of Telehealth Experience

Marshfield Clinic expands access to specialists and providers, while reducing wage loss and travel time for patients, by expanding their telehealth services. Marshfield Clinic is a Program Advisory Council (PAC) member of gpTRAC.

NEWS

The Convenience of Telehealth for Rural Patients

Recently, a University of Tennessee, Knoxville, study investigated the financial benefits of telehealth. While the benefits are stronger from the patients perspective, the rural providers and hospitals could have long-term challenges, according to this study.

Telehealth Offset Declines in Primary Care Visits, but Not Equally

The drop in care visits during the pandemic were often offset through the use of telehealth visits. However, according to the study in the Annual of Family Medicine, this offset varies by patient groups and can still depend upon various social determinants of health factors.

Telehealth Expansion And Scaling: Tough Challenges

SIU School of Medicines traditional site-based telehealth program had served patients in central and southern Illinois pre-pandemic, but once the COVID-19 public health emergency (PHE) began, SIU School of Medicines program needed to expand to see patients at home. Despite the many challenges that come with expanding a telehealth program, SIU School of Medicine providers started seeing patients at home and tried to limit the impact of the PHE on patient care as much as possible.
